In The Minds of Machines

Humanity has given birth to a new form of intelligence that surpasses our own in many ways, yet remains largely undecipherable for those who build it as well as those who use it. We have no historical precedent that can help us understand and prepare for the uncertain future setting upon us. We will most certainly see breakthroughs in science, medicine, climate control, education, manufacturing and food production. And we will most certainly experience the disruption of employment and the economy. There will be new power concentrations and new devastating tools of war. There will be AI haves and AI have-nots. Perhaps the reshaping of truth itself is waiting for us.

As AI grows increasingly smarter, more capable, and more agentic, will we become dumber, less capable, and lazier in response? Some researchers believe so. Whether you embrace AI and wish to exploit its potential — or you fear AI and its eventual negative consequences for humanity, we at MAI believe it is essential to learn and prepare best we can for our uncertain future.

The Museum of Artificial Intelligence's mission is to inform, teach, and promote discussion. We offer workshops and lectures for both beginners and the well-initiated. Our subject is the most significant invention in human history.
