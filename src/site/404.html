While you're here

The word robot was coined in 1920 by a playwright, not an engineer. Karel Čapek used it in his play R.U.R., borrowing the Czech robota — forced labour. His robots were artificial people, and the play ends with them replacing humanity altogether. We have been arguing about that ending ever since.
